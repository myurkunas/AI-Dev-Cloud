Frequently Asked Questions — General and Curriculum

About the name. Heinz College was originally the School of Urban and Public Affairs (SUPA). It was renamed the H. John Heinz III School of Public Policy and Management in 1992 after Pennsylvania Senator H. John Heinz III, and became Heinz College of Information Systems and Public Policy in 2008. The Master of Information Systems Management program was established in 1996.

How do I apply? Applications must be submitted through Heinz College's online application system; Heinz only accepts applications online. Full-time master's and Ph.D. students must matriculate in the Fall semester. There is no application fee.

Part-time programs. For working professionals with at least three years of work experience, Heinz offers two part-time programs — the Master of Public Management (MPM) and the Master of Science in Information Technology (MSIT) — with evening classes and remote learning options. These part-time programs have additional start dates throughout the year.

Deferrals. Deferrals are not common and are considered case-by-case for one academic year only, typically for military/volunteer public service or opportunities with a partner organization.

STEM designation. Several Heinz master's programs are STEM-designated (eligible for the STEM OPT extension), including AIM, MISM (and the BIDA pathway), MSISPM, MSHCA, and MSPPM. Master of Arts Management, Master of Entertainment Industry Management, Master of Public Management, Master of Medical Management, and the online MSIT are not STEM-designated.

Capstone instead of thesis. In lieu of a master's thesis, full-time master's students complete a team-based Capstone Project in collaboration with a real-world client.

What is a unit? At Carnegie Mellon, courses are counted in units rather than credits. A 12-unit course is roughly equivalent to a 4-credit-hour course elsewhere. Most Heinz courses are 12 units (a full-semester course) or 6 units (a half-semester "mini" course). The academic year is divided into four minis (two per semester), with summer minis 5 and 6 for students taking summer classes.

Transfer credits. Heinz does not accept transfer credits from institutions outside Carnegie Mellon toward its full-time degree programs. Graduate-level coursework completed in another CMU program may be evaluated case-by-case after admission. Part-time MPM and MSIT students may request evaluation of prior graduate coursework for up to 24 transfer units after accepting admission. Heinz also offers exemption exams during onboarding for students with overlapping prior coursework.

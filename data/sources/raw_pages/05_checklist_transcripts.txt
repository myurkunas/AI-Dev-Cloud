Application Checklist — Transcripts

All applicants who have completed (or will complete) their bachelor's degree in the United States must have an official transcript of their undergraduate studies submitted directly to Carnegie Mellon University's Heinz College by their undergraduate university.

All applicants who have completed (or will complete) their bachelor's degree outside the United States must have a course-by-course transcript evaluation report submitted directly to Heinz College via an approved transcript evaluation service. All international academic documents for applicants to master's programs (except the Master of Medical Management) must be verified by a transcript evaluation service.

All applicants must earn a bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited institution (or its equivalent) prior to the date they would begin their studies at Heinz College. Applicants who have not completed their bachelor's degree at the time of application may be admitted on the condition that they submit their final, official transcript, including the date of graduation, prior to the start of their first semester.

Heinz College does not have a required minimum GPA. The Admissions Committee completes a holistic review of each application, considering transcripts, test scores, recommendations, personal statement, and career goals.

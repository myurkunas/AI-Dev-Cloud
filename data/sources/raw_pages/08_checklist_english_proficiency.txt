Application Checklist — English Language Proficiency Exam Results

If your native language (mother tongue) is not English, you are required to submit an official score report from the TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo English Test. The only exceptions are for applicants who have worked or studied for five or more years in a specified list of English-speaking countries (including Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, South Africa, the United Kingdom, and the United States), or who studied for four years of their undergraduate education in the United States and earned their bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited US school.

Applicants required to submit scores must have them reported directly to Heinz College by the testing service, and must score at or above the posted minimums. Heinz College's TOEFL institution code is 2176. TOEFL and IELTS/Duolingo scores are accepted up to two years old. Applicants may submit scores from up to four valid exam dates, and Heinz College uses the highest verified score in each section. When required, applicants are strongly encouraged to take the exam at least one month prior to the application deadline.

Note: this requirement applies to international applicants only. The service answers questions about the English-language exam requirement itself but does not provide visa or immigration advice.

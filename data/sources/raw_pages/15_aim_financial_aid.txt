Affording Your AI Master's Degree (AIM) — Scholarships and Student Finances

Many AIM students receive a scholarship from Heinz College to reduce the cost of their degree. Applicants should indicate on the application for admission that they wish to be considered for merit-based support.

AI Systems Management Program Scholarships. Merit-based scholarships awarded on the basis of the application for admission, ranging from 10% to 75% of tuition. Every admitted student is considered; no additional application or documentation is required. Recipients may also earn $5,000–$6,000 per academic year through a research assistantship or part-time work for non-tuition expenses.

Other AIM scholarships and fellowships. Excellence in Technology Fellowships (a limited number of half-tuition awards); American Technology Fellowships (minimum $10,000 per semester up to full tuition, for U.S. citizens committed to IT management); the David Lingren Fellowship for Technology and Society (an additional $2,000 per semester plus a research assistantship with the Block Center); the Student Experience & Engagement (SEE) Fellowship (up to 50% tuition plus an RA role); the Deliberative Discourse Fellowship (an additional $2,000 per semester); and Pittsburgh Regional Leaders Scholarships. Some fellowships require an additional application, offered to students admitted by the January 10 deadline.

Partnership and education-partner scholarships. Applicants affiliated with a strategic partner organization, or who completed a bachelor's degree at a regional/national education partner, are eligible for partnership-based scholarships (education partners: 30% up to 100% of tuition). Indicate the affiliation on the application for admission.

Veterans. U.S. military veterans not receiving 100% tuition benefits through the VA are eligible for a scholarship of up to 30% of tuition, also available to spouses and children of veterans. Heinz also has a Fallen Heroes Scholarship Program.

Student employment and loans. Students may be eligible for Federal Work Study, Federal Community Service Work Study, or non-federal work study positions (typically $17/hour; students can access up to $18,000 in work-study awards). Federal direct loans and private education loans are available through CMU Student Financial Services.

General scholarship terms. Heinz scholarships and fellowships are typically renewable for subsequent semesters but are not guaranteed, and may be reduced or rescinded for reasons such as changing the program of study, enrolling in fewer units, extending program length, deferring admission, failing to make satisfactory academic progress, or submitting inaccurate application information. Total scholarship from all sources generally may not exceed tuition and fees. Estimated cost of attendance for AIM students is published on the CMU Student Financial Services website.

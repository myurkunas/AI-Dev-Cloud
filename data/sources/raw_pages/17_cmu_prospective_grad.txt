Carnegie Mellon University — Prospective Graduate Student Information (Office of Graduate and Postdoctoral Affairs)

Carnegie Mellon University is home to master's and doctoral programs within its seven schools and colleges, and also offers online programs and interdisciplinary programs.

Admission and application process. Each program manages its own separate admissions process, including deadlines and requirements, and this information varies by department. Prospective students are encouraged to visit the department's website for specific details. A student's qualifications are not assessed until a full application has been received and processed. Applicants are welcome to apply to more than one program, but in most cases must complete separate applications for each. All admissions-related questions — including where to submit transcripts, exam requirements, and how to set up a visit — should be directed to the department in which the applicant is interested.

Tuition and financial support. For the most part, students in the master's programs are self-supporting, and Ph.D. students are at least partially funded. Each program makes its own arrangements regarding financial support of potential graduate students. Applicants should seek information from the department of interest for their specific opportunities. More information regarding tuition and fees is available on the Student Financial Services website, and centralized fellowship and funding resources are available through the Funding Opportunities and Fellowships page.

The Heinz College of Information Systems and Public Policy is one of the seven schools and colleges at Carnegie Mellon University.

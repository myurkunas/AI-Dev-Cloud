Master of Science in Public Policy and Management (MSPPM) — Program Overview

Key program information. Program length: 2 years, 4 semesters (a 3-semester track is available for some students). Application deadlines: December 1 (Round 1), January 10 (Round 2), February 2 (Round 3). Degree earned: Master of Science. Internship: required (except the 3-semester track). STEM-designated: Yes.

The Master of Science in Public Policy and Management (MSPPM) program combines policy curriculum with instruction in statistics, economics, finance, organizational design, and presentation skills. MSPPM offers several tracks: the Flagship (Pittsburgh two-year) track, the Data Analytics track, the Washington, D.C. track, and the Fast Track (three-semester), along with policy concentrations and dual-degree options. Heinz College's Public Policy and Management program is ranked No. 8 in the United States by U.S. News & World Report.

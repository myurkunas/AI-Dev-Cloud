Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence Systems Management (AIM) — Application Process

Application deadlines. Round 1 deadline: December 1, 2026. Round 2 deadline: January 10, 2027. Round 3 deadline: February 1, 2027. Applicants for all three rounds of admission are given equal consideration for merit-based scholarships, competitive scholarships, and fellowship awards.

Admission decision timelines. Students who submit their application and supporting materials by December 1 (Round 1) receive their admission decision by the end of January. Round 2 (January 10) decisions are released by the middle of March. Round 3 (February 1) decisions are released by the end of March. Round 3 is the latest deadline for full consideration for competitive scholarship and fellowship awards.

Applying after the deadlines. Heinz continues to accept applications after the published deadlines if space remains in the incoming class; contact the Office of Admissions to apply after February 1. Late applicants receive a decision approximately four weeks after their complete application is received and processed. On June 15, 2027, the application system closes for the full-time master's incoming cohort for August 2027.

Required application materials. A completed online application (there is no application fee); official transcripts (US applicants) or a course-by-course transcript evaluation report (international applicants); a professional one- to two-page résumé; two recommendations (up to three allowed, at least one professional, submitted electronically in English); and the required essay. All applicants must earn a bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited institution before beginning studies; applicants who have not finished may be admitted on the condition that they submit a final official transcript before their first semester.

Required essay. The essay should address three questions — career goals, prior experience, and why Heinz College — with a recommended length of 700 words minimum (up to about 1,100 words). Applicants may not use AI tools such as ChatGPT to write any portion of the essay. An optional essay of up to 250 words may address gaps or weaknesses. A short video interview is optional but strongly recommended.

GRE/GMAT policy. GRE/GMAT scores are required, but applicants can qualify for a waiver by submitting one or more of: (1) evidence of prior quantitative coursework — a grade of B or higher in a college-level, credit-bearing Pre-Calculus class or higher (AP or IB Precalculus/Calculus counts if it appears as college credit on the transcript); or (2) an undergraduate degree from Carnegie Mellon University. Approval of a waiver is at the discretion of the admissions committee. Prospective AIM students are not eligible for a GRE/GMAT waiver based on work experience.

Prerequisite quantitative and technical preparation. The AIM program is quantitatively focused. Before starting classes, students must have completed college/university-level coursework in: (1) Pre-Calculus (a calculus class or higher also satisfies this), (2) Introductory Statistics, and (3) Introductory Python programming (Java and C++ are also acceptable). Applicants who do not meet all three at the time of application may be offered conditional admission and required to complete the missing courses over the summer before their first semester. Courses must be college-level at an accredited institution, for credit, and completed with a grade of B or higher; they may be taken on campus or online. Courses from Coursera or other MOOC platforms do not meet the prerequisite requirement. Admitted students who have not completed a programming course must take and pass a free online "Introduction to Python" course offered by Heinz College the summer before enrolling.

Questions about admission and prerequisites can be directed to the Office of Admissions at hnzadmit@andrew.cmu.edu or 412-268-2164.

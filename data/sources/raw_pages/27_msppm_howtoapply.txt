Master of Science in Public Policy and Management (MSPPM) — Application Process

Application deadlines (as published for the August 2026 start; at time of access this page had not yet been updated for the next cycle). Round 1 deadline: December 1, 2025. Round 2 deadline: January 10, 2026. Round 3 deadline: February 2, 2026. Heinz continues to accept applications after the published deadlines if space remains in the incoming class; contact the Office of Admissions to apply after February 2. Prospective students who do not apply by the published deadlines are still considered for merit-based scholarship support from their program. On June 15, 2026, the application system closed for the full-time master's incoming cohort for August 2026. Applicants after Round 3 receive a decision approximately four weeks after their complete application is received.

Required application materials. The required supporting materials are the essay, résumé, two recommendations, an official transcript or evaluation report for the bachelor's degree, and official test scores (if applicable).

GRE/GMAT policy. GRE/GMAT scores are required, but applicants may qualify for a waiver by submitting evidence of prior quantitative coursework — a grade of B or higher in a college-level, credit-bearing math class listed on an official transcript. Approval of a waiver request is at the discretion of the admissions committee, which assesses applicants holistically.

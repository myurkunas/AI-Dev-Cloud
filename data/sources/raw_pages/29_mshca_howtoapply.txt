Master of Science in Health Care Analytics and Information Technology (MSHCA) — Application Process

Application deadlines. Round 1 deadline: December 1, 2026. Round 2 deadline: January 10, 2027. Round 3 deadline: February 1, 2027. Applicants for all three rounds are given equal consideration for merit-based, competitive, and fellowship awards. Round 1 decisions are released by the end of January, Round 2 by the middle of March, and Round 3 by the end of March. Applications after the deadlines are accepted if space remains; late applicants receive a decision approximately four weeks after their complete application is processed. On June 15, 2027, the application system closes for the August 2027 cohort.

Required application materials. The essay, résumé, two recommendations, an official transcript or evaluation report for the bachelor's degree, and official test scores (if applicable). A completed online application is required and there is no application fee.

GRE/GMAT policy. GRE/GMAT scores are required, but applicants may qualify for a waiver by submitting one or more of: (1) evidence of prior quantitative coursework — a grade of B or higher in a college-level, credit-bearing Pre-Calculus class or higher; (2) evidence of quantitative work experience with a significant analytical component, documented and confirmed by a supervisor recommender; or (3) an undergraduate degree from Carnegie Mellon University. Unlike some Heinz programs, MSHCA does allow a waiver based on quantitative work experience. Applicants who have taken the MCAT may share those scores. Waiver approval is at the discretion of the admissions committee.

Prerequisite quantitative and technical preparation. Before starting classes, students must have completed college-level coursework in: (1) Pre-Calculus (a calculus class or higher also satisfies this), (2) Introductory Statistics, and (3) Introductory Python programming (Java and C++ are also acceptable). Applicants who do not meet all three may be offered conditional admission and required to complete the missing courses over the summer before their first semester. Courses must be college-level, for credit, and completed with a grade of B or higher; MOOC-platform courses (e.g., Coursera) do not qualify. A free online "Introduction to Python" course is offered by Heinz College the summer before enrolling.

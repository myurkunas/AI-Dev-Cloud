Master of Arts Management (MAM) — Application Process

Application deadlines (as published for the August 2026 start; at time of access this page had not yet been updated for the next cycle). Round 1 deadline: December 1, 2025. Round 2 deadline: January 10, 2026. Round 3 deadline: February 2, 2026. Applications are accepted after the published deadlines if space remains; contact the Office of Admissions to apply after February 2. Prospective students who do not apply by the published deadlines are still considered for merit-based scholarship support. Late applicants receive a decision approximately four weeks after their complete application is received. On June 15, 2026, the application system closed for the August 2026 cohort.

Required application materials. The essay, résumé, two recommendations, an official transcript or evaluation report for the bachelor's degree, and official test scores (if applicable). A completed online application is required and there is no application fee.

GRE/GMAT policy. For the current cycle, GRE/GMAT scores are optional but recommended as part of holistic review. Applicants must demonstrate quantitative skills in at least one of the following ways: (1) submitting GRE/GMAT results, and/or (2) completing a college-level algebra course (or a higher level of math) with a grade of B or higher. Applicants offered admission who have not demonstrated the necessary math proficiency are required to attend Heinz College's free Quantitative Skills Summer Program (QSSP), take a college-level algebra class, and/or take the GRE/GMAT as a condition of admission. The MAM program has no formal prerequisite coursework, but because the curriculum is quantitative, applicants are encouraged to complete statistics and pre-calculus/algebra coursework.

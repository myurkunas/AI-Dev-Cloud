Master of Entertainment Industry Management (MEIM) — Program Overview

Key program information. Program length: 2 years, 4 semesters. Application deadlines: December 1 (Round 1), January 10 (Round 2), February 2 (Round 3). Degree earned: Master's degree. Internship: required, in addition to a practicum internship during the program's second year. Location: Pittsburgh, PA in the first year and Los Angeles, CA in the second year. The MEIM program is not STEM-designated.

The Master of Entertainment Industry Management (MEIM) is a dual-city joint degree program with Carnegie Mellon University's College of Fine Arts. It combines the creative strengths of the College of Fine Arts with the management and quantitative expertise of Heinz College to prepare students for development, marketing, production, distribution, and strategy across the global entertainment industry. Students spend their first year taking core technology, analytical, and leadership classes on CMU's Pittsburgh campus (including a networking weekend in New York City), then spend their entire second year in Los Angeles completing studies while working a full-time internship in film, TV, gaming, or music. About 80% of MEIM graduates find full-time employment in the entertainment industry within three months of graduation.
